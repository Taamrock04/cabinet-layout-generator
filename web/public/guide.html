How this tool works

Lay out a control-cabinet back-plate by hand — parts, wire ducts, terminals, labels — then export a real DXF (for GstarCAD) plus PDF, PNG and SVG. Everything you see is drawn from one data model, so your exports always match the screen.

The one rule to know

It changes how you think about the tool.

Principle
The tool never invents geometry. Every line and size is exactly what you entered — it does not guess. Deterministic code draws; you review the result in CAD. If something is unknown, it's flagged for you to confirm, never silently filled in.

In practice that means: equipment can't be free-stretched with the mouse (a real part has one true size), sizes change only by typed millimetres, and the boundary/overlap checks warn you rather than quietly moving things.

The screen at a glance

  • Canvas: mouse-wheel zooms at the cursor, drag empty space to pan, Fit recentres.
  • Click an object to select it; the right panel shows its properties. Click empty space to deselect (you then see the Rows + Validation panel).

Tip
The app opens on a demo layout — feel free to drag things around and delete them to learn the controls. Use Ctrl+Z to undo anything.

Adding parts

  • From the Library: parts are grouped into bands (Power, Control, Relays, Terminals…). Click a part to drop it in, or drag it onto the plate where you want it.
  • A set of identical parts: use Add a set — pick the part, a count, and a starting tag (e.g. B101); they're placed in a row, auto-numbered.
  • Your own equipment: drag a .dxf file onto the Upload box (top of the Library). The tool measures it and asks you to confirm the size before adding it.
Remember
A marked part dimension with a * is an unconfirmed estimate — replace it with a real datasheet/DXF size before the drawing goes to the shop.

Moving & arranging

  • Drag a part on the canvas to move it. Arrow keys nudge by 1 mm; hold Shift for 10 mm.
  • Select many: Shift+click to add/remove from the selection, then move or Delete them together.
  • Rotate: select a part and press +90° in the panel (spacing uses the rotated footprint).
  • Resize: equipment keeps its true size — change it only by typing millimetres, never by stretching. (Custom parts and duct length are the exceptions; see below.)

Alignment helpers (the Align toggle)

With Align on, a dragged part snaps to the DIN-rail centreline of its row and butts up to a neighbour with the standard gap — guide lines flash to show the snap. The Snap 1 mm toggle adds a plain 1 mm grid.

Wire ducts

Add side ducts and row ducts to frame the plate and separate rows.

  • Snap to borders: with Align on, drag a duct toward a plate edge — it lands exactly on the border (all four sides).
  • Row ducts auto-span: a new + Wire duct (row) automatically stretches between the left and right side ducts — no measuring.
  • Re-fit any time: select a row duct and press Fit width to re-span it exactly between the side ducts.
  • Duct length is the one shape you may drag to resize (grab a side handle); width snaps to the standard faces.

Rows

  • The space between two horizontal ducts is a row. Each row's height is dimensioned in the right margin of the drawing.
  • Edit a row height: click its dimension on the canvas and type a new value.
  • Pack a row to line its devices up from the left duct with the standard gap; Center drops them onto the row centreline.
  • If a row's parts don't fit, you get an itemised overflow warning — nothing is silently cropped.

Stoppers & labels

End brackets for terminal rows — with an optional marker plate.

  • Stopper — a plain end bracket. Stopper with Label — a stopper plus a same-size marker plate.
  • After you add a "Stopper with Label", the label is selected so you can type its marker straight into the Tag box; it shows centred and vertical, like X-END above.
  • The two are a locked pair: drag, nudge, +90° or delete either one and both follow — but in a BOM / CAD Count Block they tally as two distinct items.

Custom parts (no CAD file yet)

A blank device you size and name yourself — perfect for a part you don't have a drawing for.

  • Click + Custom part (under Objects in the Library) to drop a blank rectangle.
  • In the panel set its Part No, Width and Height — each custom part is independent, so editing one never changes another.
  • The Tag shows above the box; the Part No is drawn centred inside and auto-fits so a long number simply shrinks to stay within the box.

Validation — warnings, not surprises

When nothing is selected, the right panel lists problems so you can fix them before exporting:

  • Overlap — two parts physically clash (highlighted red on the canvas).
  • Too tight — a part is closer to a duct than its wiring clearance allows.
  • Overflow — a row or the plate is too small for what's in it, itemised by how much.

These warn but allow — the tool never silently moves, crops or overlaps your geometry. (Coincident markers like a stopper's label are excluded, so they don't raise false overlaps.)

Exporting your drawing

FormatHowNeeds the service?
DXFPick scale 1:1 or 1:100, then Export DXFYes (ezdxf)
PDFPick paper A4/A3, then PDFNo — in browser
PNGPNGNo — in browser
SVGSVGNo — in browser
  • The DXF opens in GstarCAD 2020 with proper layers (DUCT/EQUIP/TEXT/GROUND) and prints black. Every part is a named block, so CAD's Count Block can tally them.
  • At 1:100 the geometry is drawn 1/100 size but dimension text still reads the real millimetres.
  • PDF/PNG/SVG auto-fit the chosen paper and print the resulting scale in the title line — and they work even if the DXF service is asleep.
DXF service
The status chip in the toolbar shows whether the ezdxf service is online. If it's offline, DXF is disabled but every other export still works; click the chip to retry (a free host may need a few seconds to wake).
